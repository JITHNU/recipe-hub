Caprese Skewers

A simple and elegant appetizer featuring the classic Italian flavors of tomato, basil, and mozzarella.

Ingredients

  • 1 pint cherry tomatoes
  • 1 (8 oz) package small fresh mozzarella balls (bocconcini)
  • 1 bunch fresh basil leaves
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp balsamic glaze
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Small wooden skewers

Steps

  1. Rinse the cherry tomatoes and basil leaves. Drain the mozzarella balls if they are in water.
  2. Thread one cherry tomato, one mozzarella ball, and one fresh basil leaf onto each wooden skewer.
  3. Arrange the skewers on a serving platter.
  4. Drizzle with olive oil and balsamic glaze.
  5. Season with a pinch of salt and black pepper just before serving.

Mini Quiches

Bite-sized quiches that are perfect for parties and can be customized with various fillings.

Ingredients

  • 1 sheet refrigerated pie crust, thawed
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream or milk
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/8 tsp black pepper
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheese (cheddar or Gruyère works well)
  • Fillings of your choice (e.g., cooked bacon bits, chopped spinach, sauteed onions)

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a mini muffin tin.
  2. Roll out the pie crust and use a cookie cutter or glass to cut out rounds large enough to fit into the muffin cups. Press the dough rounds gently into each cup.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, heavy cream, salt, and pepper.
  4. Sprinkle a small amount of your desired fillings and shredded cheese into each pie crust-lined muffin cup.
  5. Carefully pour the egg mixture over the fillings, filling each cup about three-quarters full.
  6. Bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the filling is set and the crust is golden brown.
  7. Let the mini quiches cool slightly before removing from the pan. Serve warm or at room temperature.
